# FHIR-53905: For FHIR Documents, clarify that only relevant references SHOULD be included

- **Description**: Currently the Documents page says:
When these resources reference other resources, the referenced resources SHOULD also be included in the bundle. Omitting referenced resources could compromise the integrity and wholeness of the document, especially when considering that documents have legal retention periods that may be longer than the life of the FHIR server where the reference(s) point to. 
In my opinion, the first sentence should be clarified to say
When these resources reference other resources, the referenced resources SHOULD also be included in the Bundle whenever those referenced resources are relevant to the document.
(or similar phrasing)

 

 

Referenced resources definitely SHOULD be included in any situation where they're needed to fully understand the parent resource (MedicationStatement.medication, DiagnosticReport.result, etc.). However, document constructors certainly shouldn't include all referenced resources (especially not recursively!) if the referenced resources are not relevant to the FHIR document, to keep the document size reasonable.

There are also various reasons why the document constructor shouldn't just remove the reference fields they don't intend to include, such as:

	Keeping a consistent resource representation in the document vs. other workflows (like REST)
	Maintaining provenance and/or digital signature validity (if constructing a document from multiple source FHIR servers)
	Maintaining resource or profile conformance (e.g. mandatory fields, even if not relevant to this specific document)
