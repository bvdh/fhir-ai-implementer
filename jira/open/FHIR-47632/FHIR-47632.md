# FHIR-47632: Versions - SHALL can be introduced in new content

## Metadata

- **Assignee**: Unassigned
- **Created**: 9/13/24
- **Description**: The section on Forward and backward compatibility rules states:
Conformance Language SHALL statements cannot be broken (though they can be clarified) and new SHALL statements cannot be introduced unless they are conditional based on version. E.g. "Instances in version x and higher of FHIR SHALL..." Changes of SHOULD to MAY and vice versa as well as introducing new MAY and SHOULD statements are permitted.
SHALL statements can be introduced in optional features. E.g. implementers SHOULD implement &lt;amazing new feature&gt;, when they do, the following &lt;wise thing&gt; SHALL be done.

Please update the sentence to state so.
- **Grouping**: [Block-Vote-6]
- **Issue Type**: Change Request
- **Key**: FHIR-47632
- **Related Page(s)**: Change Management & Versioning (versions/compatibility)
- **Related URL**: https://build.fhir.org/versions.html#f-compat
- **Reporter**: Bas van den Heuvel
- **Resolution**: Persuasive
- **Resolution Description**: Will change "new SHALL statements cannot be introduced unless they are conditional based on version. E.g. "Instances in version x and higher of FHIR SHALL...""

to

"new SHALL statements cannot be introduced unless they a) are conditional based on version (E.g. "Instances in version x and higher of FHIR SHALL..."), or b) depend on a new optional element introduced in that version."
- **Status**: Resolved - change required
- **Summary**: Versions - SHALL can be introduced in new content
