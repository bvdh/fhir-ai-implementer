# FHIR-54876: ServiceRequest - replaces should more clearly support prolongation or renewal

## Metadata

- **Assignee**: Unassigned
- **Created**: 1/19/26
- **Description**: Throughout the specification, Request.replaces does not mean "this one invalidates the previous request". However, from ome discussions and first implementation, this is not so clear. While it seems there is nothing wrong with the specification, this should have additional and more clear guidance.

Suggest to add something to the short description like "Request(s) replaced, superseded, carried or continued by this request". The comments should also be elaborated with confirmation that "replaces" supports also the case of "renovation".

Suggested use case to cover: Prescriber orders 1 box of pain killers for one week after surgery, but since patient is traveling, the prescriber orders already a continuation, which is not reimbursed, and the patient may or not act upon, depending on whether the patient has the need for the medication. 
This use case shows that the prior prescription is created at the same time at the prolongation, and while not common, if supported, it would confirm the robustness of the approach.
