The baseDefinition of all primitive types is FHIR PrimitiveType, except for xhtml. 
The baseDefinition for xhtml is FHIR Element. Why is that? 

	boolean: https://build.fhir.org/boolean.profile.xml.html
	xhtml: https://build.fhir.org/xhtml.profile.xml.html



Why is xhtml element in FHIR considered a FHIR Element, but not a FHIR PrimitiveType or even FHIR DataType?

This page shows xhtml pointing to DataType: https://build.fhir.org/datatypes.html
But it does not show xhtml pointing to PrimitiveType.

 

Is the idea that xhtml is less of a FHIR DataType than FHIR boolean or FHIR string?  https://build.fhir.org/datatypes.html#primitive But if that’s the case, then why have a StructureDefinition for xhtml and add constraints to it that otherwise don’t generally apply to XHTML? For example, that FHIR xhtml has max 0 extensions. 

Does xhtml not derive from PrimitiveType because it's not allowed to have Extensions?But Extensions are defined for Element, so it's not clear why making it derive from Element signifies that. This is already signified in the cardinality constraint for xhtml.extension. 

Instead, it should be clear that each primitive type refers to the set of values used in the context of FHIR resources, and that includes xhtml.
I propose:

	Change the baseDefinition of xhtml from Element to PrimitiveType – or at least DataType.
	Include xhtml in the list of primitive types – or explain why it's different.
