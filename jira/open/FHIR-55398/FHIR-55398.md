- **Description**: Recent discussions in IPS project have been considering the need for SHALL:populate-if-known obligations to take into consideration the clinical context and relevance associated with producing a particular document. 
The IPS Project has discussed in several calls had has agreed in certain circumstances multiple occurring elements that currently have SHALL:populate-if-know obligations may not want to be populated with ALL known occurrences due to contextual or relevance rules, which are distinct from allowed to share rules as supported in SHALL:populate obligation. 

A proposal was prepared to redefine the intent of SHALL:populate-if-known, as described in Must Support and Obligations for elements with multiple cardinality, in particular it introduced the concept of "populate if known and relevant".

However, advice was provided that an IG SHALL NOT redefine the meaning of obligation codes and if necessary a new obligation code such as 'populate-when-relevant' could be proposed.

The proposal reference above also described use cases/examples where an element with a consumer obligation of SHALL:handle could choose the occurrence it supported based on particular values it supported, types of identifiers it supported, or coding systems it supported. Although the proposed guidance got around this contextual/relevance by leveraging the vague definition of SHALL:handle and the need for considering the element data and not simply ignoring the data, it would appear to be a parallel principle of producers and consumers obligations needing to have context/relevance based conditions. 

A comment on the above mentioned proposal suggest that perhaps a applicable-code extension on the Obligation extension, similar to applicable-number, may enable an additional aspect that applies to allow obligation codes that are able to specify a controlled set of applicability criteria such as "is-relevant", "allow-to-share", etc.

I recommend that a means is provided to allow implementation guides to specify obligations that allow systems to limit the element data that SHALL be supported based on clinical context, relevance and other business rules. 

Some examples of contextual rules to limit element data support include:

	AllergyIntolerance.reaction and manifestation - a system may only want to provide the most recent reaction, most serious manifestation, or distinct set of manifestations
	Patient.generalPractitioner - a system may only want to provide a provider that is indicated as the preferred or usual primary care provider, or the provider that is local to the current location of the patient when they have a tendency to migrate at different times of year
	Patient.name - names in Australian indigenous cultures can be different depending on community they currently reside
	Patient.identifier - the primary billing identifier may be dependent on the patient being a defence personnel, veteran or civilian.
	Medication.code - the preferred coding of a medication may be dependent on the Medication being associated with a jurisdictional Medication request authorization or an international patient summary current medication list item

- **Reporter**: Heath Frankel
- **Resolution**: Persuasive with Modification
- **Resolution Description**: Add clarification text to the extensions page covering that:

Adding specific context rules within an obligation must be done via the {{usage}} property of the extension.

Similar rules can be described at a higher level through the slicing mechanism (e.g., requiring a slice with given properties) or at the profile level itself (e.g., restrictions on the context for when a profile is appropriate).

Implementation context also applies (e.g., the jurisdictional rules where a system is implemented, specific instance of a request being made) and affect the results but are not expected to be codified here.

Note that this resolution expects editorial discretion in application.

I exercised the allowed editorial discretion to draft/propose analyst-friendly refined text for inclusion on the Implementation Obligations page, with the aim of reducing ambiguity and avoiding further questions/comments.:

Create an additional subsection on the page, called: "Contextual Application of Obligations" and add the following text:

Obligations may be conditional on clinical context, relevance, or specific use cases. In such cases, the obligation code itself does not encode the conditions under which the obligation applies. Instead, these conditions are expressed through profiling mechanisms such as usage notes, constraints, slicing, or narrative guidance within a profile or implementation guide.

An obligation such as SHALL:populate-if-known applies only when the defined contextual conditions are met and does not, by itself, require population of data outside the intended scope or relevance of the element. Obligations remain bounded by the underlying model, applicable profiles, and governing policy, and should be interpreted in conjunction with any contextual rules defined elsewhere in the specification.

I'm not comfortable with that at all.  The allowed content of an element is determined by the semantics of a structure.  Obligations don't change what data needs to be conveyed.  In an IPS document, the semantics of the filtering that should occur is determined by the document type.  For IPA, there's no such filtering, which means that the expectation is all data that's allowed to be shared is exposed.  And obligations couldn't ever be used to impose limitations on what's shared (beyond potentially removing the expectation to share at all).

### 7. Lloyd McKenzie - 2/3/26
Security: Public

I don't really understand the base disposition.  It's talking about adding language to the 'extensions' page, but the discussion is only with respect to Obligation.  And we already have a different item that's clarifying the rules around how obligations work, so I'm not sure what additional clarity is desired here.

You can have obligations that are specific to a jurisdiction or a context, but that determines "when does the obligation apply", it doesn't determine "what data does it cover".  For example, if you had an obligation that said "SHALL populate medications" with a context of "psychiatry", then if you have a psychiatry app, you'd have to populate all known medications you were allowed to share - irrespective of whether they were relevant to psychiatry.  If you wanted only the medications relevant to psychiatry, you'd have to assert that filter either intrinsically through something like a List.code or Composition.code, or explicitly with a binding.

Is that what you're wanting to convey?  If so, can we just include the above example in the text on the Obligations page or extension?
