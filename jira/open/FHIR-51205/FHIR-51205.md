# FHIR-51205: Clarify author vs publisher in MetadataResource

## Metadata

- **Assignee**: Unassigned
- **Created**: 6/23/25
- **Description**: Clarify the distinction between author and publisher in the MetadataResource element. The author is a contributor to the actual content, whereas the publisher/steward is the entity primarily responsible for support and maintenance of the content.
- **Issue Type**: Change Request
- **Key**: FHIR-51205
- **Related Artifact(s)**: MetadataResource
- **Related URL**: https://hl7.org/fhir/metadataresource-definitions.html#MetadataResource.author
- **Reporter**: Bryn Rhodes
- **Resolution**: Persuasive
- **Resolution Description**: In CanonicalResource.publisher, will add to the comment "The publisher is not necessarily an entity that is involved in developing the content of the resource."

In MetadataResource.author, will change the definition from:

"An individiual or organization primarily involved in the creation and maintenance of the \{{title}}."

to

"An {color:#de350b}individual{color} or organization primarily involved in the development of the content in the \{{title}}."  (note spelling correction in individual)

Add to the comment: "An author does not necessarily have the authority to manage the actual publication of releases of the resource."
- **Status**: Resolved - change required
- **Summary**: Clarify author vs publisher in MetadataResource
- **Work Group**: FHIR Infrastructure
